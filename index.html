Iron Man

Iron Man, The fictional character seen in the comics published by the American-based comic book company Marvel Comics. Although his real name is Anthony Edward Stark, he is better known as Tony Stark. It first appeared in the 39th issue of the comic book series "Tales of Suspense", published between 1959 and 1968, in March 1963. The character's first story was written by Stan Lee and his brother Larry Lieber and plotted by Don Heck. Jack Kirby designed the character's original battle armor. After his first appearance in September 1963; He formed the Avengers team with Thor, Hulk, Ant-Man and Wasp and started his own comic book series in May 1968.

Captain America

Captain America was born on July 4, 1920 into a poor family. His mother is Sarah and his father is Joseph Rogers. He worked as a newspaper distributor for a period and studied Art School. He is a good painter. Not wanting to watch the Nazis take over Europe, Steve wanted to enlist in the army, but was rejected because his body was weak. General Chester Philips told Steve that he could join the army super soldier project and thus fight the Nazis, but would die immediately if there was a failure in the serum. Steve accepted and successfully completed this project, and it came out 4 times as strong as a human.

Hulk

Hulk is a fictional hero revealed by the comic book maker named Marvel Comics. It was created by Stan Lee and Jack Kirby. First seen in episode The Incredible Hulk # 1 (May 1962). This green giant with incredible power cannot control his emotions (anger). He has had radiation-induced mutations. The Army restarts the super soldier project that makes Captain America super powerful, but must find someone with the intelligence to do it. Thereupon, they trick Doctor Bruce Banner into believing that he made him a radiation shield. Bruce Banner is so confident about his theory that he tries it on himself. Radiation doesn't kill him, but turns him into the Hulk. When he feels that his life is in danger or gets extremely angry, he turns into the Hulk.

Thor

There are many supporting characters in the Thor comic book adapted by Marvel Comics from Germanic mythology. Many of these characters are drawn from Germanic mythology and other mythologies, as well as Marvel's own fictional characters. These characters range from superheroes to earth humans and mutants, magical creatures such as Elf, Troll, Giant and Dragon, and from aliens to gods and other cosmic beings. Other Marvel heroes are important characters. The most important characters are the gods of Asgard, who are Thor's family and people.

Ant Man

As the main character in MARVEL's comic book universe, Dr. Ant-Man, who was shaped around Henry "Hank" Pym, is a character who has survived major troubles before becoming a superhero. Hank Pym, who is married to a Russian woman, returns to his country, the USA, after the death of his wife after an attack while abroad, and he makes great inventions as an important scientist with his love pain. Hank Pym, who has the intelligence to be considered a genius in science, is a special brain with a doctorate in nanotechnology and biochemistry, and this genius will give him leading roles in the Avengers with Reed Richards, Tony Stark and Bruce Banner in the coming years.

Groot

Groot is a fictional character appearing in American comics published by Marvel Comics. Created by Stan Lee, Larry Lieber, and Jack Kirby, the character first appeared in Tales to Astonish # 13 (November 1960). The original Groot, an extraterrestrial, tree-like creature, first emerged as an invader aiming to catch humans to experiment on them.
